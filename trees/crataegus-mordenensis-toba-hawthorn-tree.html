About This Tree

Crataegus mordenensis 'Toba', commonly known as the Toba Hawthorn, is a small, ornamental tree that offers a striking visual appeal to gardens and landscapes. It is a hybrid species, known for its hardiness and beautiful floral display. In terms of its key features, the Toba Hawthorn is most notable for its double white flowers which bloom in late spring, gradually turning to a soft pink as they mature. These blooms are not only visually captivating but also attract various pollinators, making the tree a beneficial addition to any garden ecosystem.

When it comes to size, the Toba Hawthorn typically reaches a mature height of about 4 to 6 meters, with a similar spread of approximately 3 to 5 meters. This compact size makes it an excellent choice for smaller gardens or as a focal point in larger landscapes. The tree has a rounded crown and dense branching, which provides a lovely canopy of foliage. The leaves are dark green and glossy, turning shades of yellow and bronze in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

The Toba Hawthorn thrives best in well-drained soil and can adapt to a variety of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade, requiring at least 4-6 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal growth. This tree is also quite hardy and can withstand colder climates, making it suitable for a wide range of regions. Additionally, it has a moderate drought tolerance once established, although it benefits from regular watering during prolonged dry spells. Overall, the Toba Hawthorn is an excellent choice for gardeners looking to add a resilient and attractive tree to their landscape.
