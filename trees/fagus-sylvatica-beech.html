About This Tree

The Fagus sylvatica, commonly known as the European Beech, is a majestic and stately tree that is highly regarded for its beauty and versatility. This deciduous tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense canopy of lustrous, dark green leaves that turn a stunning golden brown in the autumn. The leaves are oval-shaped with wavy edges, contributing to the tree's elegant appearance throughout the growing season.

At maturity, the European Beech can reach an impressive height of 30 to 40 meters, with a spread of 15 to 25 meters, creating a broad and imposing presence in the landscape. This expansive growth makes it an excellent choice for large parks, estates, and spacious gardens. The tree’s growth habit is typically rounded or dome-shaped, and it can live for several hundred years, adding to its appeal as a long-term investment in any green space.

The European Beech thrives best in well-drained, fertile soils and displays a preference for slightly acidic to neutral pH levels. It is adaptable to both full sun and partial shade, though it tends to perform better with some protection from harsh, midday sun, especially when young. Additionally, the tree is known for its tolerance to a variety of soil types, including loam, clay, and sandy soils, provided they are not waterlogged. Notably, the European Beech produces small, triangular nuts called beechnuts, which are a valuable food source for wildlife. Due to its dense foliage, the tree also offers excellent windbreak and privacy screening capabilities, making it a versatile and valuable addition to many landscapes.
