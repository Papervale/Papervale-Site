About This Tree

The Quercus shumardii, commonly known as the Shumard Oak, is a stately and robust deciduous tree native to North America. Known for its impressive size and resilience, the Shumard Oak typically reaches a mature height of 18 to 24 meters with a spread of approximately 12 to 18 meters. This majestic oak features a broad, rounded crown that provides ample shade, making it an excellent choice for large landscapes and urban settings.

Key features of the Shumard Oak include its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves, which turn a vibrant reddish-brown in the fall, adding a splash of colour to the landscape. The acorns produced by this tree are relatively large, typically maturing in two seasons, and serve as an important food source for various wildlife species. The bark of the Shumard Oak is dark grey and develops deep furrows and ridges as the tree matures, adding to its rugged and picturesque appearance.

The Shumard Oak is quite adaptable when it comes to soil types, thriving in well-drained, acidic to slightly alkaline soils, including sandy, loamy, or clay substrates. It prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade, though optimal growth and development are achieved with ample sunlight. This tree is also relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a low-maintenance addition to suitable environments. Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make the Shumard Oak a favoured choice for both natural and urban landscapes.
