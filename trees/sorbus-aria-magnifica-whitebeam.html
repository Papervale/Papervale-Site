About This Tree

Sorbus aria 'Magnifica', commonly known as the Whitebeam, is a deciduous tree that is both striking and robust, making it an excellent choice for ornamental planting or as a feature tree in gardens and parks. One of its key features is its broad, oval leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and covered with a silvery-white down on the underside, creating a shimmering effect in the breeze. In the spring, the tree produces clusters of small white flowers that give way to red or orange berries in the autumn, adding seasonal interest and attracting wildlife.

At maturity, Sorbus aria 'Magnifica' typically reaches a height of around 10 to 15 meters and a spread of approximately 6 to 8 meters. This moderate size makes it suitable for medium to large gardens where it can provide both shade and aesthetic appeal. The tree has a rounded crown and a sturdy, upright growth habit, which contributes to its overall stately appearance.

Sorbus aria 'Magnifica' thrives in well-drained soils, preferring alkaline to neutral soil types but is quite adaptable to a range of soil conditions, including clay and sandy soils. It requires full sun to partial shade to grow well, with full sun being ideal for optimal flowering and fruiting. Notably, this tree is tolerant of urban pollution and wind, making it a resilient choice for city environments and exposed sites. Its hardy nature and low maintenance needs make it a popular choice among landscapers and gardeners looking for an attractive, trouble-free tree.
