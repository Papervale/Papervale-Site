About This Tree

The Aesculus x carnea 'Briotii', more commonly known as the Red Horse Chestnut, is a striking deciduous tree that stands out for its impressive floral display. In late spring, this tree becomes a focal point in any landscape, adorned with clusters of deep pink to red flowers that bloom in upright panicles. These vibrant blossoms not only add a splash of vivid colour but also attract pollinators, enhancing the ecological value of the area. Beyond its floral allure, the tree boasts lush, dark green foliage comprised of large, palmate leaves. These leaves form a dense and attractive canopy, offering both beauty and shade.

Reaching a mature height of 10 to 15 meters (33 to 49 feet) and a spread of 8 to 12 meters (26 to 39 feet), the Red Horse Chestnut is well-suited for parks and expansive gardens. Its rounded crown provides ample shade, creating a serene environment beneath its branches, perfect for relaxation and outdoor activities. This generous shading capability makes it an excellent addition to landscapes where shade is a valuable asset.

In terms of environmental adaptability, Aesculus x carnea 'Briotii' prefers well-drained, fertile soils, thriving particularly well in slightly acidic to neutral pH conditions. However, it is adaptable to various soil types, including clay and sandy soils. The tree flourishes in full sun to partial shade, demonstrating its versatility in different garden settings. While it benefits from ample sunlight, it can also tolerate some shade, making it a flexible option for diverse planting locations.

Additionally, the Red Horse Chestnut is relatively low maintenance, with good resistance to pests and diseases compared to other horse chestnut varieties. It is notably resilient to urban pollution, making it an ideal choice for city landscapes. When planting, it's crucial to allocate sufficient space for its large canopy to fully develop, ensuring the tree maintains its grandeur and health over time.
