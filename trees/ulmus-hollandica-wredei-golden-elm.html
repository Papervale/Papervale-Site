About This Tree

Ulmus hollandica ‘Wredei’, commonly known as the Golden Elm, is renowned for its striking, upright columnar form, making it a popular choice for adding vertical interest to landscapes. This deciduous tree is particularly celebrated for its vibrant golden-yellow foliage, which provides a stunning contrast against the more typical green shades of other trees. As the seasons change, its leaves may take on a more greenish hue, but they retain a bright, eye-catching appearance.

Typically reaching a mature height of approximately 10 to 15 meters, with a spread of about 3 to 5 meters, the Golden Elm’s compact, narrow profile makes it ideal for smaller gardens or urban settings where space is a consideration. The leaves are serrated and oval, with a distinctive golden hue that intensifies in the spring and summer, transitioning to shades of yellow or brown in autumn. The bark is smooth and grey when young, becoming more furrowed and textured with age. Inconspicuous green flowers appear in spring, followed by small, winged seeds.

Golden Elm thrives best in well-drained, fertile soils, although it can tolerate a range of soil types including clay, loam, and sandy soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade, which helps maintain its vivid leaf coloration. This tree is relatively hardy and can withstand urban pollution, making it a versatile choice for city environments. While generally robust, Ulmus hollandica ‘Wredei’ may be susceptible to Dutch elm disease, so monitoring and preventative care are advisable. It is well-suited to temperate climates and can tolerate wind exposure, making it a resilient addition to various landscapes.
