About This Tree

The Sorbus 'Olympic Flame,' commonly known as Rowan, is a deciduous tree celebrated for its striking seasonal displays. This ornamental tree typically reaches a mature height of around 6 to 8 meters with a spread of about 4 to 5 meters, making it an ideal choice for smaller gardens or as a focal point in larger landscapes.

One of the key features of the Sorbus 'Olympic Flame' is its vibrant autumn foliage. The leaves transform into a brilliant array of red, orange, and yellow hues, resembling the colours of an Olympic flame, hence the name. In spring, the tree produces clusters of small, creamy-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. By late summer, these blossoms give way to bright red berries, which are not only visually appealing but also provide a valuable food source for birds.

Sorbus 'Olympic Flame' thrives best in well-drained soils, ranging from sandy to loamy types. It has a preference for slightly acidic to neutral pH levels but is relatively adaptable to varying soil conditions. This tree enjoys full sun to partial shade, making it versatile for different planting locations. It is also relatively low-maintenance and resistant to common pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners. With its stunning seasonal changes and ecological benefits, the Sorbus 'Olympic Flame' is a remarkable addition to any garden.
