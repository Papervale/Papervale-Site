About This Tree

The Pinus halepensis, commonly known as the Aleppo pine, is a resilient and versatile tree that thrives in Mediterranean climates. This evergreen conifer can reach a mature height of 15 to 25 meters, with a spread of approximately 10 to 15 meters, making it a substantial presence in any landscape. Its canopy is typically open and irregular, providing a unique architectural element to gardens and natural areas alike.

Key features of the Aleppo pine include its long, slender needles, which are usually a vibrant green and grow in pairs. The tree produces small, oval-shaped cones that mature to a rich brown colour, adding to its visual interest throughout the year. The bark of the Aleppo pine is another notable characteristic; it starts off smooth and grey in young trees and gradually becomes thick, fissured, and orange-brown as the tree ages, providing both texture and colour contrast.

The Aleppo pine is highly adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils and can tolerate poor, rocky, and calcareous soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it an excellent choice for xeriscaping and areas prone to dry conditions. While it can handle partial shade, full sun exposure ensures the best growth and health for this tree. Additionally, the Aleppo pine is known for its ability to withstand coastal conditions, including salty winds, making it a popular choice for seaside plantings. Its resilience and adaptability, combined with its distinctive appearance, make the Pinus halepensis a valuable addition to a variety of landscapes.
