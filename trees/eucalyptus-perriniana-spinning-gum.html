About This Tree

Eucalyptus perriniana, commonly known as Spinning Gum, is a unique and visually striking species of eucalyptus native to Australia. This evergreen tree is particularly noted for its distinctive juvenile foliage, which is rounded, blue-green, and often encircles the stem, giving it a charming and whimsical appearance. As the tree matures, the leaves become more elongated and lance-shaped, adopting a more traditional eucalyptus look while maintaining their appealing silvery-blue hue.

A mature Spinning Gum can reach an impressive height of 10 to 20 meters, with a spread of approximately 5 to 10 meters, making it a suitable choice for larger gardens and landscapes. The tree's growth habit is typically upright and open, allowing for a graceful presence that doesn't dominate the surrounding space. One of the most fascinating characteristics of the Eucalyptus perriniana is its smooth, mottled bark that peels away in strips, revealing a fresh layer beneath in a palette of greens, greys, and browns.

In terms of cultivation, Eucalyptus perriniana thrives in a variety of soil types, including clay, loam, and sandy soils, provided they are well-draining. This species is quite versatile in its sun and shade preferences, flourishing in full sun to partial shade. It is also relatively hardy, tolerating frost and occasional drought once established. These qualities, along with its aesthetic appeal, make the Spinning Gum a popular choice for both ornamental and practical uses in temperate climates.
