About This Tree

Multistem specimen

The Betula utilis 'Doorenbos', commonly known as the Himalayan Birch, is a strikingly beautiful multi-stemmed tree that captures attention with its distinctive white bark. This deciduous tree is highly prized for its ornamental value, particularly in winter when its bark becomes a focal point in the garden. The bark is smooth and peels away in thin layers, revealing a fresh, white surface underneath, which makes it a standout feature in any landscape.

Mature trees of Betula utilis 'Doorenbos' can reach a height of approximately 8 to 10 meters, with a spread that ranges between 6 to 8 meters. This makes it a medium-sized tree suitable for a variety of garden settings, from expansive landscapes to more contained urban gardens. Its leaves are ovate and serrated, turning a beautiful golden-yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest throughout the year.

In terms of growing conditions, Betula utilis 'Doorenbos' thrives best in moist, well-drained soils. It is quite adaptable but prefers slightly acidic to neutral pH levels. The tree flourishes in full sun to partial shade, which helps to maintain the vibrancy of its bark. This birch is relatively hardy and can withstand cold temperatures, making it suitable for a wide range of climates. Additionally, it can be used as a focal point in garden design or planted in groups to create a striking grove. Its resilience and distinctive appearance make it a valuable addition to any landscape.
