About This Tree

Acer palmatum 'Crimson Queen', commonly recognized as the Japanese Maple, is celebrated for its breathtaking, deeply lobed foliage that cascades gracefully. Throughout the growing season, the leaves exhibit a striking crimson red, which transitions to a vibrant scarlet in the fall, creating a spectacular visual display. This slow-growing, deciduous tree typically reaches a mature height of about 2 to 3 meters (6 to 10 feet) and spreads out to approximately 3 to 4 meters (10 to 13 feet) wide, forming a broad, rounded canopy that enhances any garden environment.

The foliage of 'Crimson Queen' is not only visually appealing with its lace-like texture but also adds an elegant touch to landscapes. Its weeping and cascading growth habit makes it an ideal focal point in gardens or a charming addition to ornamental borders. This tree remains interesting throughout the seasons, showcasing its vivid leaf colours from spring through fall.

In terms of cultivation, Acer palmatum 'Crimson Queen' thrives in well-drained, slightly acidic to neutral soils, preferring moist, fertile conditions. However, it can adapt to various soil types if drainage is adequate. The tree is best suited for partial shade, which helps protect its delicate leaves from the harsh afternoon sun, although it can tolerate full sun in cooler climates if provided with sufficient water. Overall, 'Crimson Queen' serves as an exquisite choice for gardeners seeking to introduce elegance and vibrant color to their outdoor spaces, thanks to its low maintenance requirements and stunning seasonal transformations.
