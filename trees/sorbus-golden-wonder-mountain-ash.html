About This Tree

The Sorbus aucuparia 'Golden Wonder', commonly known as the Mountain Ash, is a stunning deciduous tree renowned for its ornamental value. This cultivar is particularly appreciated for its vibrant autumn display and clusters of bright yellow berries that provide a striking contrast against the dark green foliage. 'Golden Wonder' typically reaches a mature height of 8-10 meters and has a spread of approximately 4-6 meters, making it an excellent choice for medium gardens or as a focal point in landscape designs.

One of the key features of 'Golden Wonder' is its foliage, which transforms from green to a rich array of yellows and oranges in the fall, creating a spectacular seasonal display. The tree blooms in spring, producing small white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. These flowers are followed by the distinctive yellow berries that persist into the winter months, providing a valuable food source for birds and adding winter interest to the garden.

Sorbus aucuparia 'Golden Wonder' thrives in well-drained soils and is quite adaptable to a variety of soil types, including loam, clay, and sandy soils. It prefers a position in full sun to partial shade, ensuring the best growth and berry production. This hardy tree is also tolerant of urban pollution, making it a versatile option for both city and rural settings. Additionally, the Mountain Ash is relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal pruning and care once established. Its resilience and ornamental qualities make 'Golden Wonder' an excellent addition to any landscape.
