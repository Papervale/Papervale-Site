About This Tree

The Monterey Cypress, scientifically known as Cupressus macrocarpa, is a striking evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of California. This tree is highly appreciated for its unique, picturesque appearance and its adaptability to various environmental conditions. One of the most distinctive features of the Monterey Cypress is its dense, dark green foliage, which forms in a rounded, irregular shape. The bark is reddish-brown and furrowed, adding a rugged texture that enhances its visual appeal.

At maturity, the Monterey Cypress can reach a height of 20 to 25 meters, with a spread of 12 to 15 meters. Its growth habit is typically conical in youth, becoming broader and more irregular with age. The tree is well-suited to coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils ranging from sandy to loamy textures. It can tolerate a variety of soil pH levels, from slightly acidic to slightly alkaline.

Monterey Cypress prefers full sun but can also endure partial shade, making it a versatile choice for different landscape applications. One of its notable features is its remarkable resistance to strong winds and salt spray, which makes it an excellent choice for coastal planting. Additionally, this tree provides valuable habitat and food for local wildlife, including birds and small mammals. Due to its robust nature and striking form, the Monterey Cypress is often used in windbreaks, hedges, and as a specimen tree in parks and large gardens.
