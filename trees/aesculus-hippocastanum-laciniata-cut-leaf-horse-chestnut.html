About This Tree

The Aesculus hippocastanum 'Laciniata', commonly known as the Cut Leaf Horse Chestnut, is a striking deciduous tree known for its distinctive deeply lobed and serrated leaves, which provide a unique, fern-like texture. This variety stands out not only for its ornamental foliage but also for its impressive size and robust structure. At maturity, the Cut Leaf Horse Chestnut can reach a height of approximately 15 to 20 meters and a spread of about 10 to 15 meters, making it a substantial addition to any landscape.

One of the key features of this tree is its large, palmate leaves that are deeply cut into narrow, pointed lobes, giving it a finely divided appearance that is particularly eye-catching in the spring and summer. In late spring to early summer, the tree produces upright panicles of white flowers with a hint of pink or yellow, which later give way to the familiar spiky seed capsules containing the glossy brown conkers.

The Cut Leaf Horse Chestnut thrives best in well-drained, fertile soils but is adaptable to a range of soil types, provided they are not excessively dry or waterlogged. It prefers full sun to partial shade, making it versatile in various garden settings. Notably, this tree is also moderately tolerant of urban pollution, making it suitable for city parks and large gardens. However, care should be taken to plant it in a spacious area where its mature size can be accommodated. Its unique foliage and impressive stature make Aesculus hippocastanum 'Laciniata' a remarkable choice for those looking to add a distinctive feature tree to their landscape.
