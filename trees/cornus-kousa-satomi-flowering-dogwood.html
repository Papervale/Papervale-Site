About This Tree

Cornus kousa 'Satomi', widely recognized as the 'Satomi' Flowering Dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree celebrated for its stunning display of pink bracts that encircle small, inconspicuous flowers. These eye-catching bracts emerge in late spring to early summer, offering a vibrant floral show that captivates onlookers for several weeks. As the seasons transition, the 'Satomi' continues to impress with its striking autumn foliage, which transforms into rich shades of red and purple, enhancing the landscape's visual appeal. Typically reaching a mature height of 4 to 6 meters and spreading 4 to 5 meters wide, this tree creates a balanced and graceful canopy that can be a focal point in any garden.

In terms of environmental preferences, the 'Satomi' Flowering Dogwood thrives in well-drained, acidic to neutral soils, favouring rich, loamy conditions while remaining adaptable to various soil types as long as drainage is adequate. It flourishes best in full sun to partial shade, with partial shade proving beneficial in regions with hot summers, protecting the tree from the harsh effects of excessive heat and sun scorch. Notably, after its blooming period, the tree bears small, raspberry-like red fruits that attract birds and other wildlife, adding another layer of interest. The bark itself is also an attractive feature, exhibiting a mottled, exfoliating texture that provides winter interest when the tree is bare of leaves.

Due to its aesthetic charm and seasonal interest, the 'Satomi' Flowering Dogwood is an excellent choice for ornamental planting, whether as a specimen tree or within mixed borders. For gardeners seeking to enhance their landscape with elegance and colour throughout the year, this tree stands out as a superb option.
