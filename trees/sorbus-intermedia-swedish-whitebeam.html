About This Tree

Sorbus intermedia, commonly known as the Swedish Whitebeam, is a robust and versatile deciduous tree that brings a charming presence to any landscape. Typically reaching a mature height of about 10 to 15 meters, with a similar spread, this tree forms a rounded, dense canopy that offers ample shade and visual interest throughout the seasons.

The leaves of Sorbus intermedia are a distinctive feature, being broadly oval with a serrated margin and a slightly downy underside. They emerge a soft grey-green in spring, maturing to a lush dark green. Come autumn, the foliage transforms into vibrant shades of orange, red, and yellow, providing a striking display. The tree is also adorned with clusters of creamy white flowers in the spring, which are attractive to bees and other pollinators. Following the flowers, it bears orange-red berries that persist into winter, offering a valuable food source for birds and small mammals.

Sorbus intermedia is not particularly fussy about soil types, thriving in both clay and sandy soils, provided they are well-drained. It prefers a position in full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of planting locations. This species is noted for its tolerance to urban pollution and salt, making it an excellent choice for city planting or coastal areas. Its compact size and attractive features make it suitable for smaller gardens as well as public and larger landscape projects, where its year-round interest and ecological benefits can be fully appreciated.
