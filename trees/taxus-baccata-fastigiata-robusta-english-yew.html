About This Tree

The TAXUS baccata 'Fastigiata Robusta', commonly known as the English Yew, is a striking evergreen tree that boasts a narrow, columnar shape. This elegant tree typically reaches a mature height of around 8-10 meters, with a spread of approximately 1-2 meters. One of its key features is its dark green foliage, which provides a beautiful contrast against the reddish-brown bark.

English Yews thrive in well-drained soil and are adaptable to various soil types, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils. They prefer to be planted in locations with partial shade to full sun exposure. One interesting fact about the English Yew is that it produces red, berry-like fruits that are toxic if ingested, so caution should be taken if planted in areas frequented by children or pets. Additionally, the wood of the English Yew has been historically used for making longbows due to its flexibility and strength.
