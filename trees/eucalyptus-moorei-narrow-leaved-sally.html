About This Tree

Eucalyptus moorei, commonly known as Narrow-leaved Sally, is a distinctive small to medium-sized tree endemic to eastern Australia. One of its key features is its narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which provide a delicate and graceful appearance. The leaves are a vibrant green, and the tree is evergreen, maintaining its foliage throughout the year. Eucalyptus moorei typically reaches a mature height of around 10-20 meters, making it a relatively modest-sized tree compared to some of its towering eucalyptus relatives. The spread of the tree can range from 4 to 6 meters, allowing it to fit comfortably into a variety of landscapes.

This species is particularly notable for its white to cream-colored flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds. The bark of the Narrow-leaved Sally is smooth and can range in colour from grey to white, often shedding in ribbons. This shedding process leaves behind a mosaic of colours and textures on the trunk, adding an extra layer of visual interest.

Eucalyptus moorei is adaptable to a variety of soil types, though it thrives best in well-drained soils. It prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade, making it a versatile choice for different garden settings. This tree is also somewhat drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for regions with less frequent rainfall. Additionally, its relatively compact size and attractive features make Eucalyptus moorei an excellent choice for home gardens, parks, and streetscapes.
