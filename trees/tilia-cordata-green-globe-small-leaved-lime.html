About This Tree

The Tilia cordata 'Green Globe', commonly known as the Small-leaved Lime, is a beautifully compact and ornamental tree. This cultivar is distinguished by its dense, rounded crown, which gives it a neat, globular appearance, making it an excellent choice for urban landscapes and smaller gardens. The heart-shaped, dark green leaves are smaller than those of other linden species, adding to its charming and refined visual appeal.

At maturity, the Tilia cordata 'Green Globe' typically reaches a height of about 4 to 5 meters, with a similar spread, forming a well-proportioned and symmetrical shape. One of the key features of this tree is its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting bees and other pollinators, which makes it a valuable addition to any garden focused on supporting local wildlife.

The Small-leaved Lime thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, including clay, loam, and sandy soils. It prefers a position in full sun to partial shade, making it versatile in different garden settings. Additionally, Tilia cordata 'Green Globe' is known for its good resistance to urban pollution, making it an ideal choice for city parks and street plantings. Its compact size, attractive foliage, and ecological benefits make it a standout selection for those looking to add a touch of elegance and functionality to their green spaces.
