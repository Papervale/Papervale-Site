About This Tree

Multistem specimen

Magnolia stellata, commonly known as Star Magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree renowned for its stunning early spring blooms. When grown as a multistem option, this plant creates a dense, bushy form with multiple trunks, providing a striking focal point in any garden or landscape. The Star Magnolia typically reaches a mature height of around 4.5 meters and has a spread of approximately 3 meters, making it an excellent choice for smaller spaces or as an ornamental feature.

One of the key features of Magnolia stellata is its exquisite star-shaped flowers, which appear before the leaves emerge. These blossoms, usually white or occasionally pink, are composed of numerous narrow petals that create a starburst effect, adding a touch of elegance and charm to the early spring garden. The flowers are also mildly fragrant, enhancing their appeal. Following the flowering period, the tree's dark green leaves provide a lush and attractive canopy throughout the summer months, before turning a golden yellow in the fall.

Magnolia stellata thrives best in well-drained, slightly acidic to neutral soil, and it can adapt to a variety of soil types as long as they are not overly alkaline or waterlogged. This tree prefers full sun to partial shade, with more sun typically resulting in better flowering. It is relatively low maintenance once established, though it benefits from regular watering during dry spells, especially when young. The Star Magnolia is also notably hardy, able to withstand colder climates and frost better than many other magnolia species. Its compact size and stunning flowers make Magnolia stellata a versatile and valuable addition to gardens, courtyards, and urban landscapes.
