About This Tree

The Salix caprea 'Pendula', commonly referred to as the Kilmarnock Willow or Weeping Pussy Willow, is a delightful ornamental tree known for its unique appearance and relatively small size, making it a favored selection for gardens and landscaped spaces. One of the tree's most distinguishing characteristics is its elegantly weeping branches that cascade gracefully toward the ground, forming a lovely umbrella-like canopy. In early spring, the Kilmarnock Willow captivates with its charming silvery catkins that eventually transition to a vibrant yellow, providing a striking visual display before the lush green leaves emerge. The lance-shaped foliage adds to the tree's allure, showcasing a bright green color in summer that shifts to a warm yellow in autumn.

Reaching a mature height of about 1.5 to 2 meters, along with a similar spread, the Kilmarnock Willow is particularly well-suited for small gardens and container planting. This adaptable tree thrives in various soil types, especially in moist, well-drained conditions, and prefers full sun to partial shade, requiring at least 4-6 hours of direct sunlight daily to flourish. One of its appealing attributes is its low maintenance requirement; it generally needs minimal pruning, primarily to maintain its shape and to remove any dead or damaged wood.

As a grafted variety, the Kilmarnock Willow is typically cultivated on a standard rootstock to achieve its distinctive weeping form. This tree serves beautifully as a focal point in compact gardens or as part of a mixed border, enhancing the landscape with its year-round beauty and interest. Its combination of compact size and decorative features makes the Kilmarnock Willow an excellent choice for gardeners looking for a unique yet manageable tree.
