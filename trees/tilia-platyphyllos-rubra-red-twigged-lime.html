About This Tree

Tilia platyphyllos 'Rubra', commonly known as the Red Twig Lime or Red Twig Large-leaved Linden, is a striking deciduous tree notable for its vibrant red twigs and buds, which provide a splash of colour in the winter landscape. This cultivar of the Large-leaved Linden is distinguished by its broad, heart-shaped leaves, which turn a beautiful golden-yellow in the fall. The tree also produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators.

At maturity, Tilia platyphyllos 'Rubra' typically reaches a height of 20 to 25 meters and a spread of 15 to 20 meters, making it a substantial addition to any landscape. Its broad canopy provides excellent shade, making it a popular choice for parks and large gardens. The tree has a rounded to oval shape, with a sturdy trunk and a well-branched structure that adds to its aesthetic appeal.

This tree thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring moist, well-drained soils but demonstrating a high tolerance for clay and loamy soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade, ensuring it receives ample light for optimal growth. Notably, Tilia platyphyllos 'Rubra' is also relatively hardy, capable of withstanding urban pollution and a range of climatic conditions, which makes it a versatile choice for both rural and urban settings. Its ability to provide shade, attract wildlife, and withstand adverse conditions makes it a valuable and attractive addition to any landscape.
