About This Tree

Malus domestica 'Laxton's Superb' is a highly regarded cultivar of apple tree, known for its exceptional fruit quality and attractive appearance. This variety, originating from the United Kingdom, is cherished for its medium-sized, juicy, and sweet apples that have a slight tangy flavour, making them an excellent choice for both fresh eating and culinary uses.

Key features of 'Laxton's Superb' include its moderate growth habit, typically reaching a mature height of about 3 to 4 meters, and a spread of approximately 3 meters, making it suitable for smaller gardens and orchards. The tree blooms in mid to late spring, producing a profusion of delicate, white blossoms that are not only visually appealing but also beneficial for pollinators like bees. The fruit ripens around mid-October, showcasing a striking red skin with occasional russeting, and creamy, firm flesh.

In terms of cultivation, 'Laxton's Superb' thrives best in well-drained, fertile soils with a preference for slightly acidic to neutral pH levels. It performs optimally in full sun, although it can tolerate partial shade. This variety is relatively disease-resistant, particularly against common apple ailments such as scab and mildew, making it a resilient choice for both amateur and experienced gardeners. Regular pruning helps maintain its shape and encourages healthy fruit production. Overall, 'Laxton's Superb' is a versatile and rewarding apple tree that adds both beauty and bounty to any garden setting.
