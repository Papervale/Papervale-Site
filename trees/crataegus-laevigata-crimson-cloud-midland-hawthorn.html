About This Tree

Crataegus laevigata 'Crimson Cloud', commonly known as Midland Hawthorn or simply 'Crimson Cloud', is a striking ornamental tree renowned for its profuse and vibrant red blossoms. This deciduous tree typically reaches a mature height of about 5 to 7 meters, with a similar spread, making it an excellent choice for smaller gardens or as a focal point in larger landscapes.

Key features of 'Crimson Cloud' include its abundant and eye-catching clusters of single red flowers, each adorned with a distinctive white centre, that bloom profusely in late spring. These flowers are followed by small, glossy red haws (berries) which provide a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife in the autumn. The leaves are small, lobed, and dark green, turning to shades of yellow and bronze in the fall, offering a multi-seasonal display of colour.

'Crimson Cloud' thrives best in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, including clay, loam, and sandy soils, as long as they are well-drained. This tree is relatively low-maintenance and is tolerant of urban pollution, making it suitable for city environments. Additionally, it is resistant to many common pests and diseases, though it can occasionally be affected by fire blight or leaf spots. With its stunning floral display and attractive berries, the Midland Hawthorn 'Crimson Cloud' is a superb addition to any garden, providing beauty and ecological benefits year-round.
