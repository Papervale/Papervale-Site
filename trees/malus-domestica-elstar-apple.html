About This Tree

The Malus domestica 'Elstar', commonly known as the Elstar apple tree, is a delightful addition to any garden or orchard. This deciduous fruit tree is renowned for its high-yielding, crisp, and aromatic apples that are ideal for both fresh consumption and culinary uses. The Elstar apple tree is characterized by its moderately vigorous growth and compact form, making it well-suited for smaller spaces.

At maturity, the Elstar apple tree typically reaches a height of about 3 to 4 meters, with a similar spread of around 3 meters. This relatively modest size makes it an excellent choice for home gardeners looking to cultivate a manageable fruit tree. The tree boasts attractive, dark green foliage that provides a lush backdrop to the delicate white blossoms that appear in spring, signalling the beginning of the fruiting season. These blossoms are not only visually appealing but also play a crucial role in attracting pollinators, thereby enhancing fruit production.

Elstar apple trees prefer well-drained, fertile soils with a pH ranging from slightly acidic to neutral (pH 6.0-7.0). They thrive best in full sun, which ensures optimal fruit development and sweetness. While the tree can tolerate partial shade, full sunlight exposure is crucial for achieving the best fruit quality. Additionally, the Elstar variety is known for its good disease resistance, particularly against common issues like apple scab and mildew, making it a relatively low-maintenance option for growers. Overall, the Elstar apple tree is a productive and aesthetically pleasing choice, perfect for those looking to enjoy home-grown apples with minimal fuss.
