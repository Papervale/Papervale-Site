About This Tree

Syringa vulgaris 'Madame Lemoine,' commonly known as the 'Madame Lemoine' lilac, is a deciduous shrub renowned for its stunning, fragrant flowers. This cultivar is a member of the Oleaceae family and is celebrated for its double, pure white blooms that appear in late spring. The flowers are densely packed in large, conical clusters, providing a striking visual and olfactory experience in any garden setting.

At maturity, 'Madame Lemoine' typically reaches a height of 3 to 4 meters and has a similar spread, making it a substantial presence in the landscape. The shrub displays a robust, upright growth habit with heart-shaped, dark green leaves that serve as an attractive backdrop to its brilliant blooms. This lilac is an excellent choice for creating hedges, screens, or as a standalone specimen plant.

'Madame Lemoine' thrives in well-drained, fertile soils and prefers a sunny location to maximize flower production, although it can tolerate light shade. It is relatively low-maintenance, requiring only occasional pruning to remove dead or damaged wood and to promote air circulation. This lilac is also notably resistant to powdery mildew, a common issue with other lilac varieties. Overall, 'Madame Lemoine' is a superb addition to any garden, providing beauty, fragrance, and resilience.
