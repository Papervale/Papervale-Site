About This Tree

Acer pseudoplatanus, commonly known as the Sycamore Maple, is a robust and adaptable deciduous tree that stands out for its versatility and resilience in various environments. One of its most striking features is its large, palmate leaves, which are typically dark green with a coarsely toothed edge and a leathery texture. In the autumn, these leaves transform into vibrant shades of yellow or, on occasion, red/orange, providing a stunning visual display. Mature specimens can reach heights of approximately 20 to 30 meters, with a spread of about 15 to 20 meters, creating a spacious canopy that offers ample shade, making it an ideal choice for large gardens and public parks.

The Sycamore Maple's bark starts out smooth and grey during its youth but becomes rougher and fissured as the tree matures, often displaying a mottled appearance that adds to its visual appeal. In the spring, it produces inconspicuous yellow-green flowers in pendulous clusters, which eventually give way to winged seeds, known as samaras, that mature in the autumn. This tree is also beneficial for wildlife, offering food and habitat for various bird species and insects, enhancing biodiversity in its surroundings.

Acer pseudoplatanus demonstrates remarkable adaptability to different soil types, thriving in clay, loam, and sandy soils, provided they are well-drained. It can tolerate occasional waterlogging and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its tolerance to urban pollution further enhances its suitability for city landscapes and roadside plantings, making it a favoured choice for urban forestry and landscaping projects.
