About This Tree

Sorbus aucuparia 'Cardinal Royal', commonly known as the Rowan or Mountain Ash, is a deciduous tree renowned for its ornamental value and hardiness. This cultivar stands out with its upright, pyramidal form and abundant fruiting. At maturity, the 'Cardinal Royal' reaches a height of approximately 10-12 meters and a spread of around 6-8 meters, making it an excellent choice for medium-sized gardens and urban landscapes.

One of the defining features of Sorbus aucuparia 'Cardinal Royal' is its vibrant display of berries. In late summer to autumn, the tree produces clusters of bright red fruits that are not only visually striking but also provide a valuable food source for birds during the colder months. Additionally, its pinnate leaves turn a beautiful shade of yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal interest.

This cultivar thrives best in well-drained soil but is adaptable to a range of soil types, including loam, sand, and clay. It prefers full sun to partial shade, making it versatile for various planting locations. 'Cardinal Royal' is also relatively low-maintenance and resistant to many common pests and diseases, further enhancing its appeal for both novice and experienced gardeners. Its resilience and ornamental qualities make it a distinguished choice for adding year-round beauty to any landscape.
