About This Tree

The Malus baccata ‘Columnaris’, commonly known as the Columnar Siberian Crabapple, is a striking ornamental tree prized for its unique vertical growth habit. This deciduous tree is notable for its slender, upright form, making it an excellent choice for narrow spaces or as a distinctive landscape accent. At maturity, the Columnar Siberian Crabapple typically reaches a height of about 6 to 8 meters, with a spread of only 1 to 2 meters, creating a columnar silhouette that adds architectural interest to gardens and urban settings alike.

One of the key features of the Columnar Siberian Crabapple is its beautiful spring display. In early to mid-spring, the tree is adorned with an abundance of fragrant, white blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. These blossoms are followed by small, red or yellow crabapples that persist into the winter, providing a valuable food source for birds and adding winter interest to the landscape. The tree's dark green foliage turns a striking shade of yellow in the fall, further enhancing its seasonal appeal.

The Columnar Siberian Crabapple is quite adaptable and hardy, thriving in a variety of soil types, including loam, clay, and sandy soils, provided they are well-drained. It prefers full sun to partial shade, requiring at least six hours of direct sunlight per day for optimal growth and flowering. This tree is also relatively low-maintenance and resistant to common apple tree diseases, making it an excellent choice for both novice and experienced gardeners. Its compact size and vertical growth habit make it a perfect fit for small gardens, urban landscapes, and even as a living privacy screen design.
