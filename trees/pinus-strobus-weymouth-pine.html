About This Tree

The Weymouth Pine, also known as the Eastern White Pine (Pinus strobus), is a majestic conifer native to eastern North America. Renowned for its graceful, feathery appearance, this tree features long, slender needles grouped in bundles of five, giving it a soft, almost wispy texture. The needles are bluish-green, adding a touch of colour variation that enhances its visual appeal throughout the seasons.

In terms of size, the Weymouth Pine is impressive, reaching mature heights of 30 to 50 meters (approximately 98 to 164 feet). Its spread, or the width of its canopy, can extend to about 12 to 15 meters (approximately 39 to 49 feet), providing ample shade and making it a significant presence in any landscape. The tree grows relatively quickly and can live for several centuries under the right conditions, making it a long-term investment for parks and large properties.

The Weymouth Pine thrives best in well-drained, sandy loam soils but is adaptable to various soil types, provided they are not overly compacted or waterlogged. It prefers full sun to partial shade, with optimal growth occurring in areas that receive plenty of sunlight. This pine is also known for its resilience to cold temperatures and can withstand the harsh winters typical of its native range. Notably, its wood is highly valued for its straight grain and lightweight properties, making it a favourite for lumber and construction purposes. Additionally, the Weymouth Pine plays a crucial ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
