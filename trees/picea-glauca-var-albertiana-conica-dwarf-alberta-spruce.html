About This Tree

The Dwarf Alberta Spruce, scientifically known as Picea glauca var. albertiana 'Conica', is a highly sought-after coniferous tree celebrated for its dense and conical shape as well as its vibrant green foliage. This compact evergreen typically reaches a mature height of 2 to 3 meters and spreads between 1 to 1.5 meters, giving it a tidy, pyramidal form that is ideal for various landscape designs. Its soft, bright green needles retain their colour throughout the year, providing year-round greenery, even during the winter months. With a slow growth rate, the Dwarf Alberta Spruce is particularly well-suited for small gardens and container planting, as it naturally develops a symmetrical shape without the need for extensive pruning.

In terms of environmental preferences, this tree thrives in well-drained, acidic to neutral soils and can adapt to a range of soil types, provided they do not retain excess water. It flourishes best in full sun, requiring at least six hours of direct sunlight each day, although it can tolerate partial shade. The Dwarf Alberta Spruce is a versatile addition to both formal and informal garden settings, often used as an accent plant, in rock gardens, or even as a living decoration during the holiday season. Its manageable size and adaptability make it an excellent choice for urban landscapes and smaller outdoor spaces.
