About This Tree

The Quercus castaneifolia 'Green Spire', commonly known as the Upright Chestnut-Leaved Oak, is a striking deciduous tree renowned for its vertical growth habit and elegant foliage. This oak variety features glossy, dark green leaves that resemble those of the chestnut tree, contributing to its unique and appealing aesthetic. The leaves have a serrated edge and a slightly leathery texture, turning a rich golden-brown in the autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
This tree typically reaches a mature height of 15 to 20 meters, with a spread of approximately 6 to 8 meters, making it an excellent choice for narrow spaces or as a focal point in larger gardens. Its upright, columnar form makes it particularly suitable for lining streets, creating natural screens, or serving as a striking vertical element in a mixed planting scheme. The 'Green Spire' is also known for its sturdy structure and resistance to strong winds, making it a reliable choice for various environments.
The Upright Chestnut-Leaved Oak thrives in well-drained soil and can adapt to a range of soil types, from acidic to slightly alkaline. It prefers full sun to partial shade, ensuring optimal growth and vitality. Additionally, this tree is relatively low-maintenance once established, requiring minimal pruning and exhibiting good resistance to pests and diseases. Its adaptability and aesthetic qualities make Quercus castaneifolia 'Green Spire' an excellent addition to both urban and rural landscapes, providing beauty and structure throughout the seasons.
