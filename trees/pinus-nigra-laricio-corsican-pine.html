About This Tree

The Corsican Pine (Pinus nigra subsp. laricio), also known as the Laricio Pine or Black Pine, is a stately and resilient evergreen tree renowned for its striking appearance and adaptability. This tree is native to the mountainous regions of Corsica and Southern Italy, where it thrives in a variety of conditions.

Key features of the Corsican Pine include its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The needles, which grow in pairs, are long and slender, giving the tree a graceful, feathery appearance. The bark is another distinctive characteristic, being thick, rough, and deeply fissured, often with a dark, almost black hue that contrasts beautifully with its needles.

At maturity, the Corsican Pine can reach an impressive height of 30 to 40 meters with a spread of about 8 to 12 meters. This robust growth makes it a popular choice for timber production as well as a striking ornamental tree in larger gardens and parks. The Corsican Pine is highly adaptable when it comes to soil types, thriving in well-drained sandy, loamy, or clay soils. It has a particular affinity for slightly acidic to neutral pH levels. Regarding sunlight, the Corsican Pine prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade, making it a versatile addition to various landscape settings. Notably, this tree is also quite drought-tolerant once established, adding to its appeal for use in areas with less consistent rainfall.

Overall, the Corsican Pine is a magnificent tree that offers both aesthetic appeal and practical benefits, making it a valuable addition to diverse environments.
