About This Tree

The Malus domestica 'Beauty of Bath', commonly known as the Beauty of Bath apple, is a delightful early-season variety renowned for its attractive appearance and delicious flavour. This apple tree is a deciduous, flowering plant that produces medium-sized fruits with a distinctive red and yellow striped skin, often with a rosy blush. The flesh is crisp, juicy, and has a tangy-sweet flavour, making it a favourite for fresh eating.

In terms of growth, the Beauty of Bath apple tree typically reaches a mature height of about 3 to 4 meters, with a similar spread of approximately 3 to 4 meters. This compact size makes it suitable for smaller gardens or as part of an orchard. The tree blossoms in mid to late spring with charming white to pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. The apples ripen early, often ready for picking in late July to early August, making it one of the earliest apple varieties to harvest.

The Beauty of Bath apple tree thrives best in well-drained, fertile soil with a neutral to slightly acidic pH. It prefers a sunny position to ensure the best fruit quality and yield. While it can tolerate partial shade, full sun exposure is ideal for optimal growth and fruit production. Regular watering and annual pruning are recommended to maintain the tree's health and productivity. Notably, this variety is partially self-fertile, but planting it near other apple trees can enhance pollination and fruit set. Overall, the Beauty of Bath is a charming and productive addition to any garden, offering early-season apples that are a joy to grow and eat.
