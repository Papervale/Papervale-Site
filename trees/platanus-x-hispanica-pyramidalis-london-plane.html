About This Tree

The London Plane, scientifically known as Platanus × hispanica 'Pyramidalis', is a splendid and resilient tree, a popular choice for urban environments due to its robustness and notable aesthetic appeal. This deciduous tree is characterized by its distinctive pyramidal shape, which provides a structured and elegant silhouette, making it an excellent choice for avenues and large landscapes. The bark of the London Plane is particularly striking, with a patchwork appearance that exfoliates in large plates to reveal a mosaic of creamy, green, and brown tones underneath. This unique feature not only adds visual interest but also helps the tree in its natural defence against pollution.

At maturity, the 'Pyramidalis' variety of the London Plane typically reaches a height of approximately 20-25 meters, with a spread of around 8-12 meters. This substantial size makes it a commanding presence in any setting, offering ample shade and a grand canopy. The leaves are large, lobed, and reminiscent of maple leaves, turning golden-brown in the autumn before they fall. In spring, the tree produces small, spherical flower clusters, which later develop into spiky seed balls that persist through winter.

The London Plane is exceptionally adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types including clay, loam, and sand, provided they are well-drained. It is tolerant of both acidic and alkaline soils, making it a versatile addition to diverse planting schemes. Full sun is preferred for optimal growth, although it can tolerate partial shade. Notably, this tree is highly resistant to pollution and urban stress, making it a favoured choice for city planting. Its resilience to diseases such as anthracnose and its ability to withstand heavy pruning further contribute to its popularity and enduring presence in metropolitan areas.
