About This Tree

The Tetradium daniellii, commonly known as the Bee-bee Tree, is a magnificent deciduous tree known for its environmental benefits and ornamental appeal. This medium-sized tree typically reaches a mature height of 7 to 12 meters and boasts a similar spread, forming a rounded and appealing canopy. One of its most striking features is its profusion of small, creamy-white flowers that bloom in late summer, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators, hence its name. These flowers are followed by clusters of red berries that add visual interest and provide food for wildlife.

The Bee-bee Tree thrives in a variety of soil types, including loamy, sandy, and clay soils, as long as they are well-drained. It is not particularly fussy about soil pH, although it prefers slightly acidic to neutral conditions. This tree is quite adaptable and can flourish in both full sun and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for various landscape settings. However, it tends to flower more profusely when planted in full sun.

Notable for its relatively fast growth rate and resilience, Tetradium daniellii is a low-maintenance tree that can withstand urban pollution, making it an excellent choice for city environments. Additionally, its bark, which is smooth and grey when young but develops shallow fissures with age, adds to its ornamental value. Whether planted as a standalone specimen or in groupings, the Bee-bee Tree is a valuable addition to any garden or urban landscape, providing aesthetic beauty and ecological benefits.
