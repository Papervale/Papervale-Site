About This Tree

The Heptacodium miconioides, commonly known as the Seven Son Flower of Zhejiang, is a captivating ornamental tree that hails from China. This deciduous tree is admired for its unique and striking appearance, as well as its ability to add year-round interest to gardens and landscapes. Typically, the Seven Son Flower reaches a mature height of 4.5 to 6 meters and spreads to about 3 to 4.5 meters. Its multi-stemmed, vase-like structure creates an elegant and airy silhouette, making it a standout specimen in any setting.

One of the most notable features of Heptacodium miconioides is its clusters of fragrant, creamy-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. These flowers are not only attractive but also a magnet for pollinators like bees and butterflies. After the flowering period, the tree produces small, red-purple fruit that further enhance its visual appeal. Additionally, the bark of the Seven Son Flower peels in thin strips, revealing a lighter layer beneath, which provides an interesting texture and colour contrast during the winter months when the tree is otherwise bare.

The Seven Son Flower is quite adaptable and can thrive in a variety of soil types, ranging from well-drained loam to clay. It prefers full sun to partial shade, making it versatile for different landscape conditions. While it is fairly low-maintenance, it benefits from occasional pruning to maintain its shape and encourage healthy growth. Whether planted as a focal point in a garden or as part of a mixed border, Heptacodium miconioides offers a unique combination of beauty and resilience that is sure to delight gardeners and nature enthusiasts alike.
