About This Tree

PRUNUS domestica 'Opal' / Opal Plum

Prunus domestica 'Opal', commonly known as the Opal Plum, is a delightful deciduous fruit tree that stands out for its attractive appearance and delicious fruit. This tree is well-regarded for its early-ripening plums, which are renowned for their sweet flavour and versatility. The Opal Plum tree typically reaches a mature height of about 3.5 to 4.5 meters, with a spread of approximately 3 to 4 meters, making it a manageable size for many gardens and orchards.

One of the key features of the Opal Plum is its stunning springtime display of white blossoms, which not only adds a touch of beauty to the landscape but also attracts pollinators such as bees. The fruit itself is medium-sized, with a deep purple skin and golden-yellow flesh that is both juicy and aromatic. This cultivar is self-fertile, which means it doesn't require a second tree for pollination, though planting more than one can increase fruit yield.

The Opal Plum tree thrives best in well-drained, fertile soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH. It prefers full sun to promote optimal growth and fruit production, though it can tolerate some light shade. This tree is relatively hardy and can withstand colder climates, making it suitable for a variety of regions. Regular watering and annual pruning help maintain its health and productivity, ensuring an abundant harvest each year. With its combination of aesthetic appeal and delectable fruit, the Opal Plum is a fantastic addition to any garden.
