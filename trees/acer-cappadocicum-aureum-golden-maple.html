About This Tree

The Acer cappadocicum 'Aureum', commonly known as the Golden Cappadocian Maple, is a standout specimen tree that brings a splash of colour to any landscape. This deciduous tree is celebrated for its striking foliage, which emerges a vivid shade of golden-yellow in spring. As the seasons progress, the leaves transform into a lighter green, providing a bright and airy feel, before turning to shades of orange and red in the autumn. This dynamic colour progression makes the Golden Cappadocian Maple a focal point in gardens across the seasons.

Mature specimens of Acer cappadocicum 'Aureum' can reach heights of approximately 10 to 12 meters, with a spread that mirrors its height, creating a rounded, balanced canopy that is as wide as it is tall. This size makes it an excellent choice for medium to large gardens, where it can be given the space to truly showcase its beauty. Its bark, too, adds to its appeal, maturing to a smooth, creamy grey that provides winter interest.

For optimal growth, the Golden Cappadocian Maple prefers a position in full sun to partial shade, highlighting its adaptability to various garden aspects. It is not overly demanding regarding soil types, thriving in well-drained, fertile soils, but it does appreciate a moist environment. Avoiding waterlogged conditions is key to its health. Its tolerance of air pollution makes it a viable option for urban gardens, adding to its versatility. With these care considerations in mind, the Acer cappadocicum 'Aureum' is a resilient and enchanting tree, offering year-round interest to gardens and landscapes.
