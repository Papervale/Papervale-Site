About This Tree

The Pyrus salicifolia 'Pendula', commonly known as the Weeping Silver Pear, is a graceful and ornamental deciduous tree that adds a touch of elegance to any garden or landscape. This tree is particularly noted for its weeping habit and striking silver-grey foliage. The slender, willow-like leaves create a delicate, airy canopy that gently cascades down, providing a soft, ethereal look throughout the growing season. In spring, the tree produces small, white, cup-shaped flowers that are mildly fragrant and attract pollinators, adding to its ornamental appeal.

When fully mature, the Weeping Silver Pear typically reaches a height of around 4 to 6 meters with a spread of approximately 3 to 4 meters. This modest size makes it an excellent choice for smaller gardens or as a focal point in larger landscapes. The tree's bark is smooth and grey, which contrasts beautifully with the silvery leaves and adds winter interest when the tree is bare.

The Weeping Silver Pear thrives in well-drained soil and is adaptable to a range of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils. It prefers a sunny position but can tolerate partial shade, although its foliage may not be as vibrant in shadier spots. This tree is relatively low-maintenance and drought-tolerant once established, making it an excellent choice for gardeners seeking a striking yet easy-to-care-for addition to their landscape. Additionally, its resistance to urban pollution makes it suitable for city environments. Overall, the Pyrus salicifolia 'Pendula' is a versatile and enchanting tree that brings beauty and charm to a variety of garden settings.
