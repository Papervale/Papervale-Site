About This Tree

Hamamelis × intermedia 'Diane', commonly known as Witch Hazel, is a remarkable deciduous shrub that captivates garden enthusiasts with its stunning winter blooms and vibrant fall foliage. This variety is particularly noted for its eye-catching red flowers, which emerge in late winter or early spring, injecting a burst of colour into the garden during a time when most other plants are dormant. Typically, 'Diane' reaches a mature height of 3 to 4 meters, with a similar spread, making it an ideal selection for smaller gardens or as a focal point in larger landscapes.

This cultivar thrives in well-drained soils that range from acidic to neutral, preferring those rich in organic matter. While it is adaptable to various soil types, it is important to ensure that the planting area does not accumulate stagnant water. In terms of sunlight, 'Diane' flourishes best in full sun to partial shade. In hotter climates, providing some afternoon shade can help protect the plant from the harsh midday sun, allowing its flowers and foliage to retain their vibrant colours.

One of the notable features of this Witch Hazel is its winter interest, as the vivid red flowers provide a striking visual display in the coldest months. Additionally, in autumn, the leaves transform into a brilliant array of yellow, orange, and red hues, adding seasonal charm before the plant sheds its foliage for winter. Furthermore, once established, Hamamelis × intermedia 'Diane' requires minimal maintenance, making it a low-maintenance option that needs little pruning to maintain its shape and health. Overall, this versatile and hardy shrub enhances any garden with its unique beauty throughout multiple seasons.
