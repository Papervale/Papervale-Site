About This Tree

Taxus baccata 'Fastigiata Aurea', commonly known as the Golden Irish Yew, is a striking conifer known for its vibrant golden-yellow foliage and elegant, narrow columnar form. This cultivar of the English Yew is particularly noted for its architectural beauty, making it an excellent choice for creating vertical accents in gardens and landscapes. The foliage maintains its golden hue throughout the year, adding a touch of brightness even in the winter months.

At maturity, the Golden Irish Yew typically reaches a height of 4 to 8 meters, with a spread of approximately 1 to 2 meters. Its upright, dense growth habit makes it ideal for use as a focal point, hedge, or as part of a mixed border. The slow-growing nature of this tree means it requires minimal maintenance, and it can be easily pruned to maintain its shape and size.

Taxus baccata 'Fastigiata Aurea' thrives in a variety of soil types, provided they are well-drained. It prefers slightly acidic to neutral soils but is adaptable to alkaline conditions as well. This yew does well in full sun to partial shade, although too much shade may cause the golden foliage to become less vibrant. It is also tolerant of urban pollution, making it a versatile choice for both rural and urban gardens. With its striking color and form, the Golden Irish Yew is a remarkable addition to any landscape, providing year-round interest and structure.
