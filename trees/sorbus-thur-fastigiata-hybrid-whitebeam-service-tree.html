About This Tree

The Sorbus thuringiaca 'Fastigiata,' commonly known as the Hybrid Whitebeam or Service Tree, is a striking deciduous tree. This cultivar is notable for its upright, columnar growth habit, making it an excellent choice for narrow spaces and urban gardens. As it matures, 'Fastigiata' can reach a height of approximately 8 to 12 meters, with a spread of about 4 to 6 meters, providing a stately yet manageable size for various landscape applications.

One of the key features of the Hybrid Whitebeam is its attractive foliage. The leaves are dark green and deeply lobed, presenting a silvery underside that adds a shimmering effect when the wind rustles through the branches. In spring, the tree produces clusters of small white flowers, which are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees. These flowers give way to small, red-orange berries in the autumn, providing a splash of colour and a food source for birds.

Sorbus thuringiaca 'Fastigiata' thrives in a range of soil types, including clay, loam, and sandy soils, as long as they are well-drained. It prefers full sun to partial shade, making it versatile for various garden settings. Additionally, this tree has good drought tolerance once established, although it performs best with consistent moisture. Its resilience to pollution and urban conditions makes it a popular choice for city planting schemes. Overall, the Hybrid Whitebeam is an elegant and robust tree that enhances any landscape with its structural form and seasonal interest.
