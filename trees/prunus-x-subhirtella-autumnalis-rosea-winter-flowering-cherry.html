About This Tree

The Prunus x subhirtella 'Autumnalis Rosea', commonly known as the Winter Flowering Cherry, is a delightful deciduous tree that adds a splash of colour to the colder months. This tree is renowned for its delicate, semi-double, pale pink blossoms that appear sporadically from late autumn to early spring, often offering a surprising and welcome sight in the winter landscape. The flowers are small but numerous, creating a soft pink cloud effect against the bare branches.

Key features of the 'Autumnalis Rosea' include its graceful, arching growth habit and its slender, dark green leaves which turn to vibrant hues of yellow and bronze in the fall before shedding. This cherry tree typically reaches a mature height of about 6-8 meters and has a similar spread of around 6 meters, making it an excellent choice for smaller gardens or as a specimen tree in larger landscapes.

In terms of growing conditions, the Winter Flowering Cherry prefers well-drained soil and can thrive in a range of soil types, from sandy loam to clay. It flourishes best in full sun but can tolerate partial shade. This tree is relatively hardy and can withstand cold temperatures, though it benefits from protection against harsh winds. With its enchanting winter blooms and manageable size, Prunus x subhirtella 'Autumnalis Rosea' is a charming addition to any garden, bringing beauty and interest during the dreariest months of the year.
