About This Tree

The Ginkgo biloba ‘Blagon,’ commonly known as the Maidenhair Tree, is a stunning deciduous tree that captivates with its unique, fan-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow fall colour. This cultivar is particularly noted for its symmetrical, pyramidal form which adds a touch of elegance to any landscape. At maturity, the Ginkgo biloba ‘Blagon’ typically reaches a height of around 10-15 meters and has a spread of approximately 6-8 meters, making it a moderate-sized tree suitable for urban and suburban settings.

One of the key features of the Maidenhair Tree is its distinctive foliage. The leaves are bright green and have a two-lobed, fan shape, reminiscent of the maidenhair fern, which gives the tree its common name. In the autumn, these leaves turn a brilliant yellow before falling, providing a striking seasonal display. The tree is also known for its resilience and longevity, often living for hundreds of years and withstanding urban pollution, pests, and diseases.

Ginkgo biloba ‘Blagon’ prefers well-drained soil and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils. It does best in full sun but can tolerate partial shade. This adaptability, combined with its low maintenance requirements, makes it an excellent choice for gardeners and landscapers looking for a hardy, visually appealing tree. Additionally, the Ginkgo biloba is dioecious, meaning there are separate male and female trees; ‘Blagon’ is a male cultivar, which means it does not produce the messy and malodorous seeds that can be found on female trees. Overall, the Maidenhair Tree is a remarkable addition to any garden or landscape, offering beauty, resilience, and a touch of ancient history.
