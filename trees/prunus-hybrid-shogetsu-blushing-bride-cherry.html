About This Tree

The Prunus Hybrid 'Shogetsu', endearingly known as the 'Blushing Bride', is a captivating ornamental cherry tree that enchants with its prolific floral display. This tree is renowned for its abundant clusters of large, double flowers that unfurl in a pinkish-white hue during mid to late spring. As the blossoms mature, they gradually transition to a pristine white, offering a mesmerizing visual transformation that delights all who witness it. This particular cherry tree is a favourite among garden enthusiasts for its ability to create a dramatic and enchanting landscape feature.

When fully grown, the 'Shogetsu' cherry tree typically attains a height of about 4 to 8 meters (13 to 26 feet) with a similar spread, forming a broad, vase-shaped canopy with gracefully arching branches. This makes it an ideal choice for gardens and landscapes seeking a striking floral display. The tree's foliage is a spectacle in itself, beginning as bronze in spring, turning lush green in summer, and finally transitioning to brilliant shades of orange and red in Autumn. The smooth grey bark adds an element of textural contrast, particularly noticeable in winter. While the tree may produce small, inconspicuous fruits, these are not considered a primary feature, as the blossoms remain the star attraction.

Prunus Hybrid 'Shogetsu' thrives in well-drained soils, showing a preference for loamy, sandy, or clay soils with a slightly acidic to neutral pH. This cherry tree flourishes best in full sun to partial shade, requiring at least four to six hours of direct sunlight daily to ensure optimal growth and flowering. In areas with hot summers, it benefits from afternoon shade to protect it from excessive heat stress. Overall, the 'Shogetsu' cherry tree is an exquisite addition to any garden, offering a spectacular floral display and vibrant seasonal foliage. Its adaptability to various soil types and its preference for sun to partial shade make it a versatile and rewarding choice for gardeners, whether novice or seasoned.
