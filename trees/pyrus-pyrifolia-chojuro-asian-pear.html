About This Tree

Pyrus pyrifolia 'Chojuro,' commonly known as the Asian Pear or Nashi Pear, is a delightful fruit-bearing tree that offers both ornamental beauty and delicious, crisp fruit. This deciduous tree is renowned for its distinctive round, golden-brown pears, which have a crisp texture and a sweet, mildly tart flavour. The 'Chojuro' variety, in particular, is celebrated for its excellent storage qualities and high yield.

At maturity, the 'Chojuro' Asian Pear tree typically reaches a height of 4 to 6 meters and spreads to about 4 to 5 meters. Its growth habit is rounded and dense, making it an attractive choice for small orchards or even as a specimen tree in a garden setting. In the spring, the tree is adorned with beautiful white blossoms, which not only enhance its aesthetic appeal but also attract pollinators, essential for fruit set.

The 'Chojuro' Asian Pear thrives best in well-drained, loamy soils with a pH range from slightly acidic to neutral. It prefers full sun exposure, which ensures optimal fruit production and flavour development. While it can tolerate partial shade, fruit yield may be reduced. Regular watering is necessary, especially during dry periods, to maintain healthy growth and fruit quality. This variety is also relatively resistant to fire blight, a common pear disease, making it a hardy and reliable choice for home gardeners and orchardists alike. Overall, Pyrus pyrifolia 'Chojuro' is a versatile and rewarding tree, perfect for those seeking both an ornamental and productive addition to their landscape.
