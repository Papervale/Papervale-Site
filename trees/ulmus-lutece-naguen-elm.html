About This Tree

Ulmus 'Lutece,' also known as the Lutece Elm, is a robust and resilient hybrid elm tree known for its impressive resistance to Dutch elm disease. This deciduous tree features a broad, upright growth habit, making it an excellent choice for urban planting and landscaping. It is revered for its attractive, glossy green foliage that turns a striking golden-yellow in the autumn, providing a vivid seasonal display.

Key features of Ulmus 'Lutece' include its mature height and spread, which typically reach around 15 to 18 meters in height and 10 to 12 meters in spread. This moderate to large-sized tree develops a well-balanced, symmetrical canopy that offers ample shade, making it ideal for parks, streets, and large gardens. The bark is greyish-brown and becomes deeply fissured with age, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

Ulmus 'Lutece' thrives in a variety of soil types, including loamy, sandy, and clay soils, if they are well-drained. It prefers full sun to partial shade, ensuring optimal growth and health. Additionally, this elm is highly tolerant of urban pollution, wind, and drought, making it a versatile and low-maintenance option for many landscapes. With its disease resistance, adaptability, and striking appearance, Ulmus 'Lutece' stands out as a remarkable tree for enhancing green spaces.
