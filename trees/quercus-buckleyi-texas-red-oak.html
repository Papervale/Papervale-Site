About This Tree

Quercus buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's Oak or Texas Red Oak, is a deciduous tree native to the south-central United States. This tree is particularly valued for its adaptability and stunning autumn foliage, which showcases a vibrant palette of red, orange, and yellow leaves. Mature Buckley's Oaks typically reach heights between 10 to 15 meters and have a broad, rounded canopy that spreads approximately 9 to 12 meters, making it a prominent feature in any landscape.

The foliage of Buckley's Oak is deeply lobed and shines with a glossy green hue during the growing season, transforming into a brilliant display of colours in the fall. Additionally, the tree produces small acorns that serve as a crucial food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is another noteworthy characteristic; it is dark brown and develops a rugged texture as the tree ages, contributing visual interest even in winter months.

In terms of environmental preferences, Quercus buckleyi thrives in well-drained soils and shows tolerance for a variety of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and rocky conditions. While it prefers full sun for optimal growth, tolerating partial shade is also within its capabilities, as long as it receives at least six hours of direct sunlight each day. Overall, Buckley's Oak is an excellent choice for those seeking a hardy and attractive tree for their landscape, thanks to its resilience and striking seasonal changes.
