About This Tree

The Acer 'Pacific Sunset', commonly known as the Norway Maple, is a stunning deciduous tree that offers a blend of aesthetic appeal and practical landscape utility. This hybrid tree combines the brilliant fall colours of the Norway Maple with the fine-textured leaves of the Shantung Maple, resulting in a visually striking addition to any garden or urban space.

Key features of the Acer 'Pacific Sunset' include its vibrant foliage, which transitions from a lush green in the spring and summer to a spectacular display of yellow, orange, and red in the autumn. The tree's leaves are palmately lobed, offering a delicate and intricate appearance. At maturity, the 'Pacific Sunset' Maple typically reaches a height of about 9-12 meters and a similar spread of around 7-9 meters, making it a moderately-sized tree suitable for various landscape applications.

This tree thrives in well-drained soil and is adaptable to a range of soil types, from sandy loam to clay. It prefers full sun to partial shade, ensuring that it receives at least 4-6 hours of direct sunlight daily for optimal growth and vibrant fall colour. Additionally, the Acer 'Pacific Sunset' is relatively low maintenance, exhibiting good tolerance to urban pollution and drought conditions once established. These attributes make it an excellent choice for homeowners, city planners, and landscape architects looking for a resilient and visually appealing tree to enhance their outdoor spaces.
