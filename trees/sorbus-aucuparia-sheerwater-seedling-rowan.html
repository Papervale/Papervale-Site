About This Tree

The Sorbus aucuparia 'Sheerwater Seedling', commonly known as the Rowan or Mountain Ash, is a striking deciduous tree that is well-suited to a variety of landscapes. This cultivar is particularly noted for its upright growth habit, making it an excellent choice for urban environments or smaller gardens where space is limited. At maturity, the 'Sheerwater Seedling' typically reaches a height of around 8 to 10 meters, with a spread of approximately 4 to 6 meters. Its compact form and tidy appearance make it an attractive feature throughout the year.

Key features of the Sorbus aucuparia 'Sheerwater Seedling' include its pinnate leaves, which consist of multiple small, serrated leaflets that turn a stunning shade of orange-red in the autumn. In spring, the tree produces clusters of small white flowers that are not only visually appealing but also attract a variety of pollinators. These flowers give way to bright red berries in the late summer and autumn, providing a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife.

This tree is highly adaptable and can thrive in a range of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and clay soils, as long as they are well-drained. It prefers a position in full sun to partial shade, which helps ensure the best display of autumn color and fruit production. The 'Sheerwater Seedling' is also relatively low-maintenance and resilient to various environmental stresses, making it a robust choice for both novice and experienced gardeners. Its ability to withstand urban pollution further enhances its suitability for city planting.
