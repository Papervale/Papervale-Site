About This Tree

Acer pseudoplatanus 'Leopoldii', commonly referred to as the variegated sycamore maple, is a remarkable deciduous tree celebrated for its ornamental foliage and resilience. This particular cultivar of sycamore maple is highly regarded in landscape design due to its distinctive leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions. The most notable feature of 'Leopoldii' is its striking variegated leaves, which are broad and lobed, predominantly green with irregular creamy-white patches that create a captivating visual contrast. As autumn approaches, the foliage transforms into a beautiful golden-yellow before it falls. The tree's bark begins smooth and grey in its youth, gradually becoming rougher with age and developing scaly plates. In spring, 'Leopoldii' produces small yellow-green flowers, which give way to winged seeds, known as samaras, in Autumn; these seeds spin as they descend, earning them the nickname "helicopters."

Mature specimens of Acer pseudoplatanus 'Leopoldii' can reach heights of approximately 10 to 15 meters, with a broad, rounded crown spreading around 6 to 10 meters. This tree is adaptable, thriving in various soil types, including clay, loam, and sandy soils, though it prefers well-drained, fertile ground. It flourishes in full sun to partial shade, requiring several hours of direct sunlight daily to maintain its vibrant leaf variegation. Notably, 'Leopoldii' exhibits excellent wind resistance, making it suitable for exposed locations. It is generally resistant to pests and diseases, although vigilance for common issues like aphids and leaf spot is advisable. Due to its attractive foliage and manageable size, this tree is often utilized as a specimen in gardens and parks, enhancing the elegance and diversity of any landscape. In conclusion, Acer pseudoplatanus 'Leopoldii' is a versatile and visually appealing tree, merging aesthetic beauty with functional benefits, making it a favoured choice among gardeners and landscapers.
