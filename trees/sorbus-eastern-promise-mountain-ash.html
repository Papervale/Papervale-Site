About This Tree

Sorbus 'Eastern Promise,' commonly known as Mountain Ash, is a striking deciduous tree admired for its vibrant autumn foliage and clusters of colourful berries. This medium-sized tree typically reaches a mature height of around 5 to 7 meters, with a spread of approximately 4 to 6 meters, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes looking for a touch of seasonal drama.

One of the key features of Sorbus 'Eastern Promise' is its stunning display of foliage. During the autumn months, its leaves transform into a spectacular array of reds, oranges, and purples, providing a brilliant splash of colour before they fall. In the spring, the tree produces delicate white flowers that give way to clusters of pink berries in the late summer and autumn. These berries are not only visually appealing but also serve as a valuable food source for birds and wildlife.

Sorbus 'Eastern Promise' is quite adaptable in terms of soil requirements, thriving best in well-drained soils. It prefers a position in full sun to partial shade, ensuring it receives adequate light to develop its vibrant autumn hues. The tree is also relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal pruning and exhibiting good resistance to pests and diseases. Its elegant form and seasonal interest make it an excellent addition to any garden, providing year-round beauty and ecological benefits.
