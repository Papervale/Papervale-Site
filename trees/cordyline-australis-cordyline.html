About This Tree

Cordyline australis, commonly known as the cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant that adds an architectural element to gardens with its tall, palm-like appearance. This tree can reach a mature height of 12 to 20 meters, with a spread of approximately 2 to 4 meters, making it a captivating focal point in any landscape. Its long, narrow leaves form dense clumps at the top of slender trunks, creating a lush, tropical feel. During the summer, Cordyline australis produces fragrant, creamy white flowers that attract pollinators, followed by small, white to bluish berries.

This versatile plant thrives in a variety of soil types, including sandy, loamy, and well-drained soils, and it prefers a sunny position to partial shade to reach its full potential. While it is relatively drought-tolerant once established, it benefits from regular watering during prolonged dry spells.

Cordyline australis is not only admired for its ornamental qualities but also for its resilience, as it can withstand coastal conditions and temperatures as low as -5°C. With its striking form and adaptability, the cabbage tree is a popular choice for both urban and rural gardens alike.
