About This Tree

The Tilia x mongolica 'Harvest Gold', also known as the Mongolian Lime or Harvest Gold Linden, is a striking and resilient deciduous tree that offers several appealing features for landscapers and gardeners. This tree is especially noted for its brilliant golden-yellow fall foliage, which provides a vibrant splash of colour as the seasons change. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, creating a lush, textured canopy that is both attractive and functional, offering ample shade during the warmer months.

In terms of size, the 'Harvest Gold' typically reaches a mature height of approximately 10-15 meters and has a spread of about 6-10 meters. This moderate size makes it suitable for a variety of settings, including urban environments, parks, and larger garden spaces. The tree has a pyramidal to rounded form, which adds an elegant and structured look to any landscape.

One of the standout features of Tilia x mongolica 'Harvest Gold' is its adaptability to different soil types. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils but is also tolerant of clay and sandy conditions, provided they are not waterlogged. This tree prefers full sun to partial shade, making it versatile in terms of planting location. Additionally, it is known for its pest resistance and hardy nature, able to withstand harsh winters and urban pollution. These qualities, combined with its stunning aesthetic appeal, make the 'Harvest Gold' Linden a valuable addition to any landscape design.
