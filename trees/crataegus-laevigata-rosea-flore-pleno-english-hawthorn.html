About This Tree

The Crataegus laevigata 'Rosea Flore Pleno', commonly known as the English Hawthorn 'Rosea Flore Pleno', is a deciduous tree renowned for its charming appearance and ornamental value. This cultivar is particularly noted for its double pink flowers, which bloom profusely in late spring, providing a stunning display that can brighten any landscape. The blossoms are followed by small red fruits, that persist into winter, adding another layer of seasonal interest. The foliage consists of lobed, glossy green leaves that turn to shades of yellow and bronze in the fall.

Key features of the English Hawthorn 'Rosea Flore Pleno' include its compact and rounded form, making it an excellent choice for smaller gardens or as a feature tree in larger landscapes. At maturity, this tree typically reaches a height of around 5-8 meters and a spread of approximately 4-7 meters. Its dense branching structure and thorny stems provide excellent shelter and nesting sites for birds, enhancing its value for wildlife.

This tree is quite adaptable and can thrive in a variety of soil types, from loamy to clay soils, as long as there is good drainage. It prefers a position in full sun to partial shade, where it can receive at least a few hours of direct sunlight each day. The English Hawthorn 'Rosea Flore Pleno' is also relatively drought-tolerant once established, although it benefits from regular watering during prolonged dry spells. Its resilience and low maintenance needs make it a popular choice for urban settings and mixed borders. Overall, the Crataegus laevigata 'Rosea Flore Pleno' is a versatile and attractive addition to any garden, combining beauty with ecological benefits.
