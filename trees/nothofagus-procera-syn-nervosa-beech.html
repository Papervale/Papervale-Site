About This Tree

Nothofagus procera, also known by its synonym Nothofagus nervosa, is an impressive deciduous tree native to the temperate forests of the southern hemisphere, particularly in South America. This majestic tree can reach mature heights of 30 to 40 meters, with a spread ranging from 15 to 25 meters, making it a substantial presence in any landscape. The tree is characterized by its broad, rounded canopy and smooth, grayish bark that becomes furrowed with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with serrated margins, providing a lush, green appearance during the growing season and turning vibrant shades of yellow and orange in the fall.

Nothofagus procera thrives in well-drained, fertile soils, preferring loamy to sandy textures that retain moisture without becoming waterlogged. It is moderately adaptable to different soil pH levels, although it performs best in slightly acidic to neutral conditions. This tree is relatively shade-tolerant when young, but it prefers full sun to partial shade as it matures, ensuring optimal growth and development. It is also known for its resilience to cold temperatures, making it suitable for a range of temperate climates.

Notable features of Nothofagus procera include its ecological importance and aesthetic value. It provides habitat and food for various wildlife species, contributing to biodiversity. It is also a popular choice for large parks and gardens due to its impressive size and ornamental foliage. Additionally, its timber is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it useful for construction and woodworking. Whether planted for ecological, aesthetic, or practical purposes, Nothofagus procera is a tree that offers numerous benefits and stands out in any landscape.
