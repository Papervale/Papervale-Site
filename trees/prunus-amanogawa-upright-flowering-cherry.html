About This Tree

The Prunus 'Amanogawa', commonly known as the Upright Flowering Cherry, is a striking ornamental tree admired for its slender, columnar growth habit and profusion of delicate blossoms. This deciduous tree is a wonderful addition to gardens and landscapes where space is limited, thanks to its narrow form. In the spring, it bursts into bloom with an abundance of pale pink, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, adding both beauty and ecological value to the environment.

Key features of the Prunus 'Amanogawa' include its mature height and spread, which make it particularly versatile for various planting schemes. Typically, this tree reaches a mature height of 5 to 8 meters, with a spread of about 1.5 to 2 meters. Its vertical stature makes it an excellent choice for small gardens, urban settings, or as a focal point in mixed borders. The foliage is also noteworthy, as the tree's leaves emerge a fresh green colour in spring, turn a lush, dark green in summer, and eventually transform into warm shades of yellow, orange, and red in the autumn.

For optimal growth, the Prunus 'Amanogawa' prefers well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of soil types, from sandy to loamy, provided they are not waterlogged. It thrives best in full sun to partial shade, ensuring it receives ample light for robust flowering and healthy foliage development. This tree is relatively low-maintenance, though it benefits from occasional pruning to maintain its elegant shape and remove any dead or damaged branches. With its graceful form and seasonal interest, the Upright Flowering Cherry is a delightful choice for gardeners seeking to add vertical interest and exquisite blooms to their outdoor spaces.
