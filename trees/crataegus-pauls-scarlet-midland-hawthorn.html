About This Tree

Crataegus 'Paul's Scarlet', commonly known as the Midland Hawthorn or simply 'Paul's Scarlet', is a striking deciduous tree renowned for its vibrant double red flowers that bloom profusely in late spring. The tree, which belongs to the Rosaceae family, is often chosen for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of settings.

One of the key features of 'Paul's Scarlet' is its mature height and spread. This tree typically reaches a height of 4 to 6 meters and has a similar spread, making it a manageable size for gardens and urban landscapes. Its compact form and rounded crown make it an excellent choice for small spaces or as a focal point in larger landscapes. The leaves are dark green and lobed, providing a lush backdrop to the stunning clusters of red flowers.

Midland Hawthorn is quite adaptable when it comes to soil types, thriving in well-drained soils ranging from clay to sandy loams. It prefers full sun to partial shade, ensuring that it receives at least a few hours of sunlight daily. Besides its aesthetic appeal, 'Paul's Scarlet' is also valued for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements. It can withstand urban pollution and is resistant to many common pests and diseases. In autumn, the tree produces small red berries that attract birds and wildlife, adding to its ecological value. Overall, Crataegus 'Paul's Scarlet' is a versatile and beautiful addition to any landscape.
