About This Tree

Sorbus hupehensis 'Pink Pagoda', commonly known as the Chinese Mountain Ash, is a captivating ornamental tree that offers an array of visual delights throughout the seasons. This deciduous tree is well-regarded for its elegant form and prolific display of pinkish-white berries, which mature to a stunning pink hue in the autumn. The foliage is equally attractive, with pinnate leaves that turn a vibrant mix of orange, red, and purple in the fall, providing a spectacular seasonal show.

Key features of the 'Pink Pagoda' include its mature height and spread. This tree typically reaches a height of 6 to 8 meters and a spread of 4 to 6 meters, making it a suitable choice for medium-sized gardens or as a standout specimen tree. The canopy is gracefully rounded, offering a pleasing shape that adds structure to the landscape.

Notable features of Sorbus hupehensis 'Pink Pagoda' include its adaptability to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it versatile for different garden settings. Additionally, this tree is relatively low-maintenance and can tolerate urban pollution, which adds to its appeal for city gardens. With its blend of aesthetic charm and practical resilience, 'Pink Pagoda' is a superb choice for adding year-round interest to any garden.
