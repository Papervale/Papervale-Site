About This Tree

PARROTIA persica / Persian Ironwood Tree

Parrotia persica, also known as Persian Ironwood, is a deciduous tree that belongs to the family of Hamamelidaceae. This tree is native to Iran, but it has become a popular ornamental tree in many parts of the world due to its beautiful features. It is a slow-growing tree that can reach up to 50 feet in height and 30 feet in width when mare.

One of the most notable features of Parrotia persica is its stunning bark which peels away in several layers to reveal a patchwork of colours ranging from deep browns to silvery greys. The leaves of this tree are also quite attractive, with an oval shape and a glossy green colour that turns to shades of yellow, orange, and red in the fall.
Parrotia persica is a hardy tree that prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It can grow in a variety of soil types, including loam, clay, and sand. This tree is also tolerant of drought and can withstand harsh winters.

Overall, Parrotia persica is an excellent choice for landscaping due to its unique bark, beautiful foliage, and low maintenance requirements. It can be used as a specimen tree or planted in groups to create a stunning display of colour and texture
