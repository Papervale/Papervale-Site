About This Tree

Clearstem

The Pinus sylvestris, commonly known as the Scots Pine, is a resilient and versatile coniferous tree native to Europe and Asia. When grown as a clearstem, this tree is characterized by its tall, straight trunk devoid of lower branches, which showcases its reddish-brown bark that becomes increasingly flaky and rugged with age. The Scots Pine boasts bluish-green, twisted needles that are typically 4-7 cm long, arranged in pairs, and remain evergreen throughout the year.

This majestic tree can reach a mature height of up to 25-30 meters with a spread of approximately 6-8 meters, making it an impressive addition to landscapes requiring vertical interest and structure. The upper canopy of the Scots Pine tends to develop an irregular shape, often forming a picturesque and open crown that allows light to filter through.

Scots Pine thrives in well-drained, sandy soils but is notably adaptable to a variety of soil types, including acidic and moderately alkaline conditions. It prefers full sun exposure, which is essential for its optimal growth and development. This species is also known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a suitable choice for various climates. Notably, the Scots Pine is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
