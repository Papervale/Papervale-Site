2. Data we collect and why

Contact form enquiries

When you submit our contact form we collect your name, email address, and the message you send us. We use this to respond to your enquiry. The legal basis is your consent (Article 6(1)(a) UK GDPR), which you give by choosing to send the message. Form submissions are processed and stored by Netlify (see Section 5).

Shop orders

When you place an order through our shop we collect your name, delivery address, email address, phone number, and order details. We need this to fulfil your order and comply with our contract with you (Article 6(1)(b)). Where required, we also retain transaction records to meet legal accounting obligations (Article 6(1)(c)). Order data is processed by Ecwid by Lightspeed (see Section 5). Payment card details are handled directly by Ecwid's payment processor and are never seen or stored by us.

Website hosting & server logs

Our website is hosted by Netlify. When you visit any page, Netlify's servers automatically record your IP address, browser type, referring URL, and pages accessed. This is a standard part of operating a web server and is processed on the basis of our legitimate interests in keeping the site secure and functioning (Article 6(1)(f)). We do not run any separate analytics service.

3. Cookies

This website does not set any first-party cookies. However, the Ecwid shop widget on our Shop page and individual product pages sets cookies that are strictly necessary to operate your shopping basket and maintain your session. These cookies do not track you across other websites and do not require your consent under the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) because they are essential to the service you have requested.

A full list of cookies set by Ecwid is available in Ecwid's Cookie Policy.

4. How long we keep your data

  • Contact form messages — retained in Netlify Forms until manually deleted; we aim to delete enquiries no longer needed within 12 months.
  • Order records — retained by Ecwid for the duration of our account and as required by UK tax law (currently 6 years).
  • Server logs — retained by Netlify for up to 30 days in the ordinary course of operations.

5. Third-party data processors

We use the following services to operate this website. Each acts as a data processor on our behalf and is bound by appropriate data processing agreements.

Ecwid by Lightspeed

Ecwid provides our online shop. It processes customer order data, manages the shopping cart, and handles payment processing. Ecwid may transfer data to servers in the United States under standard contractual clauses. For full details see Ecwid's Privacy Policy.

Netlify

Netlify hosts this website and receives contact form submissions. Netlify operates servers primarily in the United States and the European Union, with data transfers covered by standard contractual clauses. For full details see Netlify's Privacy Policy.

6. International transfers

Ecwid and Netlify may process data outside the UK and the EEA (European Economic Area). Where this occurs, we rely on standard contractual clauses approved by the relevant supervisory authority as the transfer mechanism, as permitted under UK GDPR Article 46.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR (and EU GDPR where it applies) you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure — ask us to delete your data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing.
  • Restriction — ask us to restrict processing in certain circumstances.
  • Data portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Object — object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info@papervaletrees.com. We will respond within one calendar month.

8. Right to complain

If you are based in the United Kingdom and believe we have not handled your data correctly, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Website: ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint
  • Phone: 0303 123 1113

If you are based in the Republic of Ireland or another EU member state, you may instead contact the Data Protection Commission (DPC) at dataprotection.ie.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The date at the top of the page will always reflect the most recent revision. For significant changes we will post a notice on the website.
