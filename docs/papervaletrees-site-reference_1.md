### GROW STRONG `/grow-strong.html`

**Headline:** Papervale Trees - Grow Strong
**Subheading:** Rooted in passion. Grown with purpose.

**Strong Roots section:**
Specially designed corrugated slotted pots for all container-grown trees. Promotes air pruning, encouraging dense, fibrous root systems — essential for quick establishment and healthy growth.

**Shaping for Success:**
Formative pruning is a cornerstone of our growing process.
- Spring pruning promotes vigorous new growth.
- Summer tying ensures strong apical dominance and ideal stem taper.
Together, these techniques ensure each tree grows with a well-balanced canopy and structural integrity.

**Growing Sustainably:**
- Peat-free growing media from day one
- Reduced chemical use through integrated pest management
- Green manure crops to enrich the soil naturally
- Innovative weed control and low-input fertilisation methods

"We don't grow trees for short-term impact. We grow legacies."

**British Standard Quality:**
All practices align with BS8545 — the British Standard for nursery tree cultivation and planting in the landscape.

**UKISG Certified:**
Northern Ireland's first tree nursery to receive UKISG (UK & Ireland Sourcing Guarantee) certification.
Certifies trees are genuinely home-grown, not imported.
Ensures full traceability, strengthens biosecurity, and supports local horticulture.

### PLANTING & AFTERCARE GUIDE `/planting--aftercare-guide.html`

**Intro:** Here are some simple tips to help your tree get off to the best possible start.

**Before Planting:**
Place in a light and airy spot (out of direct sunlight). Set pot on a saucer of water for up to two days to rehydrate and acclimatise.

**Planting Your Tree:**
1. Dig a square hole with straight sides, about twice the width of the pot (square encourages outward root growth)
2. Loosen the base — break up soil at the bottom
3. Position the tree — top of root ball level with or slightly above surrounding ground
4. Backfill and firm — remove air pockets, give stability
5. Mulch — keep 1m circle free from grass/weeds; apply 5cm mulch each spring, not touching stem

**Supporting Your Tree:**
- Staking: one stake at two-thirds height on windward side, outside root ball
- Tree Ties: firm but loose enough for gentle movement (encourages strong trunk)
- Tree Guards: for areas with rabbits, deer, or strimmers
- Keep support for 1–2 years, then remove

**Watering:**
- Water thoroughly right after planting
- During establishment (first 2 years): deep soak once or twice per week
- 10–20 litres per watering depending on tree size and soil type
- Check soil a few inches down before watering — slightly damp but not waterlogged
- Maintain 5cm mulch layer around base (not touching stem)

**Feeding:**
- First year: avoid strong fertilisers
- From second spring: slow/controlled release fertiliser in early spring
- Balanced NPK (e.g. 10-10-10) works for most trees
- Organic option: well-rotted compost or manure
- Annual routine: renew mulch layer and top up fertiliser each spring

### PLANT `/plant.html`

**Headline:** Papervale Trees home-grown from seed to sale

**Body:**
We take pride in producing over 90% of our trees at our nursery.
We believe the horticulture industry has become over-reliant on importing plants from mainland Europe.

The unregulated importation of plant material has brought devastating pests and diseases into the UK, threatening our native flora and fauna.

We take the time to master the skills of sowing seeds, taking cuttings, budding, and grafting throughout the year to ensure we have our own supply of home-grown trees.

We strongly support the ethos of sourcing trees locally and selecting trees whose origin can be fully traced.

**Health and Vitality section:**
In 2011, the UK government published a White Paper aimed at reducing peat removal from endangered wetland habitats.

Jonathan trialed various combinations of peat alternative growing media, including composted bark, wood fibre, coir, and green waste, until the best blend was found. This growing media mix produces exceptionally good root systems.

Before any tree leaves the nursery, it is checked for health and vitality via a non-invasive measurement of leaf chlorophyll content — a measurable indicator of the plant's physiological condition.
